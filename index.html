I'm a Computer Engineering student at PUP Manila with a strong interest in systems where hardware and software meet — from microcontrollers sending data to cloud dashboards, to sensors driving real-world automation. My thesis, FAHRENCOOL, is an IoT-enabled cryotherapy and thermotherapy system built around an Arduino, Peltier modules, PID control, and a web-based monitoring dashboard — a project that reflects my belief that good engineering is end-to-end.

Outside of embedded work, I design and simulate enterprise network topologies in Cisco Packet Tracer, covering routing protocols, VLAN segmentation, and ACL-secured perimeters. I enjoy the challenge of working across the full stack — from circuit traces all the way up to cloud APIs — and I'm always looking for problems worth solving.
